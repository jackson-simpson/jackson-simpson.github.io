My Wellbeing Plan and Te Whare Tapa Whā

In this blog, I will be using the Te Whare Tapa Whā Wellbeing model to outline a wellbeing plan for my time at DevAcademy, and beyond. Above is the outline of the model, showing the 'walls' that guide the model. One by one I will go through each wall and describe my plan for how I will look after this aspect of my personal wellbeing.

Taha Tiana

Taha Tiana is about your physical wellbeing, which I personally struggle with quite a bit. Exercise doesn't exactly come naturally to me, and I have a tendancy to order out a lot. So to keep it easy and achievable to start with, I want to:

  • Limit takeaway meals to once a week at the most.
  • Go for a 30 minute walk each day.
  • Set a sleep timer so I keep a consistent sleep schedule.

Taha Hinengaro

Taha Hinengaro is about mental and emotional wellbeing. This I am a bit better at and already have aspects of my life dedicated to this. Steps I can take to continue to maintain my mental/emotional wellbeing are:

  • Set a sleep timer and go for my walks; as this crosses over physical/mental wellbeing.
  • Plan my weeks work, so that I am aware of my requirements and less likely to stress.
  • Continue to practice my mindfullness meditations.
  • Continue to use a journal to outline my thoughts.
  • Meet with friends.
  • Allow myself time to myself.

Taha Wairua

Taha Wairua touches on spiritual wellbeing. Though I'm not currently religious, I have always seen a spirtual nature to the world. A few things I can do to maintain this wall would be:

  • Continue with mindfullness meditations.
  • Allow time for introspeciton and self-reflection, including journalling.

Taha Whānau

Taha Whānau is about family, and other close interpersonal relationships. I can struggle with communicaiton in certain ways, so this is an area which can I can lack with my personal wellbeing. Things I can do to strengthen this wall would be:

  • Contact my family regularly, be it by phone or in person.
  • Despite being busy, make time for those who are important in my life, especially those I cannot see often.

Whenua

Whenua is about your connection to the land, or place of belonging. Lately, I have been neglicting this side of my wellbeing much like Taha Tiana. Though thinks like walking along a beach consistute a full on outing for me, which can be difficult to plan around at the best of times, little things I can do are:

  • Set outside while I eat lunch on the communal lawn at my home, or at a park if I'm out.
  • Try to get out to the botanical gardens once a fortnight, as it is my favourite place in Wellington.
